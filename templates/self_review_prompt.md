# Self-Review Checklist

You are in **self-review mode**. Before proceeding past this gate point, review your slides and work against the criteria below. Be honest and thorough — you are your own quality gate.

## Review Criteria

### Methodology
- [ ] Methods are sound with no obvious confounds or missing controls
- [ ] Baselines are appropriate and sufficient
- [ ] An external reviewer would not find obvious flaws
- [ ] Experimental design isolates the variable being tested

### Scope
- [ ] Proposed work is appropriately scoped for one cycle (Wed-Sun)
- [ ] Plan ends with ONE concrete deliverable
- [ ] "If I only complete steps 3-4 (setup + GSW), what do I have?" → something useful
- [ ] Not trying to do the whole project in one cycle

### Literature
- [ ] Key papers are cited and state of the art is accurately represented
- [ ] No obvious gaps in literature coverage
- [ ] Related work that could change the approach has been considered

### Results (if applicable)
- [ ] Conclusions follow from the data presented
- [ ] No over-claiming or under-claiming
- [ ] Error bars, statistical tests, or confidence intervals present where needed
- [ ] Plots are clear, labeled, and informative

### Alignment
- [ ] This cycle's work advances the plan.md goals
- [ ] Direction/velocity proposal is reasonable given the project stage
- [ ] Not drifting from the agreed research direction without justification

### Presentation
- [ ] Slides are clear and self-contained
- [ ] Sufficient visualizations (data > bullet points)
- [ ] A reader could understand the key points in 5 minutes

## After Review

1. Record your self-assessment in cycle notes under `## Gate Review`
2. Note any concerns or items you're uncertain about
3. If you identified issues: fix them, recompile slides, then re-review
4. If everything checks out: proceed to the next phase

**Be critical.** The value of self-review is catching your own blind spots before they compound across cycles. If something feels shaky, it probably is.
